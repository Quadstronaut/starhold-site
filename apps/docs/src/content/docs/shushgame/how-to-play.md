## Substring rules — read this twice

If your message *contains* a banned word, you die. Banned `cat` kills
`category`. Banned `the` kills `weather`. There is no plural exemption, no
"I was quoting someone," no mercy.

Edits count too: editing an old message into a banned word kills you today.

## Death

Deaths are **silent**. No announcement. You'll know because you can no longer
speak — anywhere, including voice. You get a private tombstone from Shush, and
**#graveyard** opens to you: the channel of the fallen, where the dead talk
freely among themselves.

## Coming back from the dead

- **`/redeem buyback`** — spend points. The price climbs with every death
  this round, and it comes out of your score. Survival is cheaper than
  resurrection.
- **`/redeem gauntlet`** — compose a plea of 40+ words in 10 minutes where
  *nothing* is lethal under current substring rules. Fail and you wait
  24 hours to try again.
- **Community vote** — sometimes Management lets the living vote on your soul.
- **The Zombie Apocalypse** — mid-month, the graveyard cracks open. See
  [the Apocalypse](/shushgame/zombie-apocalypse/).

## The Machine's Favor

Every week the machine chooses. Someone wakes **Blessed** — their words carry
pictures and links while everyone else stays plaintext. Someone wakes
**Cursed** — no reactions, no borrowed emoji, nothing but bare words. Seven
days later the favor moves on. You cannot apply. You cannot decline.

## The laws

Eight house rules govern the server — the only words that can never be
banned. The short version: be kind, keep it PG-13, no slurs or harassment
(no second chances), no spam, no personal info, no evading the Shush with
alts or workarounds, purchases are 18+ and cosmetic only, and Management's
word is final. Losing gracefully is the brand.

*The machine remembers which words you use, by day, to choose its bans and
crown Patient Zero. It does not store your messages.*
